# Case Study: The "Urgent Scholarship" Phishing Attack

## 1. What is Phishing?
Phishing is a type of social engineering attack where a cybercriminal masquerades as a trusted entity—such as a university 
administration, a bank, or a popular service provider—to deceive victims into  revealing sensitive information. 
This typically includes login credentials, credit card numbers, or UPI details.

## 2. How the Crime Happens (Step-by-Step)
* **Step 1: The Bait:** The attacker sends a mass email to students with a subject line like "Urgent: Action Required for
Scholarship Disbursement".
* **Step 2: The Deception:** The email uses the official college logo and professional language to create a sense of urgency,
claiming the student will lose their funding if they don't "verify" their account within two hours.
* **Step 3: The Malicious Link:** The victim clicks a button in the email that leads to a fake login page that looks identical
to the official university portal.
* **Step 4: The Theft:** Once the student enters their username and password on the fake site, the attacker captures the data
in real-time.
* **Step 5: Exploitation:** The criminal uses these credentials to access the student’s real account to steal personal identity
data or financial information.

## 3. Target Audience
The primary targets are **college students** who are often waiting for official communications regarding fees, grades, or 
internships. 
Attackers exploit the high-stress environment of academic deadlines to bypass a student's natural caution.

## 4. Consequences
The consequences for the victim include **Identity Theft**, where the attacker can pose as the student to commit further crimes, and 
**Financial Loss** if the account is linked to payment portals. 
Academically, the student may lose access to their course materials and personal records.

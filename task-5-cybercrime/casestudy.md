Case Study: Phishing Attacks on College Students

Definition: Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers pose as legitimate institutions—like a university administration or a bank—to trick individuals into providing sensitive data, such as login credentials or UPI PINs.

How it Happens:  
1. The Bait: A student receives an urgent email appearing to be from the "University IT Department" claiming their account will be    deactivated unless they "verify" their details immediately.
2. The Hook: The email contains a link to a fake login page that looks identical to the official college portal.
3. The Catch: Once the student enters their username and password, the attacker captures the credentials in real-time.
4. The Exploit: The attacker uses the credentials to access the student's personal information, academic records, or linked payment apps.

Target Audience: College students are often targeted because they frequently use digital portals and may react quickly to "urgent" administrative alerts.

Consequences: This can lead to identity theft, unauthorized financial transactions, and loss of access to critical academic resources.
